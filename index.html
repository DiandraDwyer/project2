Hair Texture

For any hair care regimen, it’s important to understand your hair texture, or how straight, curly or kinky your hair is. Different hair textures require different hair care routines!

This is important because the more curly your hair texture, the less your scalp’s natural oils will slide down your hair strands and moisturize them. This means, the curlier your hair is, the more your hair will need to be moisturized.

Click on the left and right arrows to learn more about hair textures!

3A Hair Texture

3A hair is wavy and has a distinctive "S" shape. The waves are typically the width of sidewalk chalk. General hair care for 3A hair includes:

  • Wash hair every 2 to 3 days
  • Shampoo mainly at hair roots
  • Deep condition for 5 to 10 minutes
  • Co-wash (wash with conditioner only) once a week, in place of shampoo
  • Colmb from the tips of your hair and slowly work your way up your hair to your roots
  • Detangle knots with your fingers and a wide-tooth colmb

3B Hair Texture

3B hair is characterized by loose, large coils. The coils each tend to be the size of a sharpee and the body of the hair is full and dense. General hair care for 3A hair includes:

  • Wash hair every 3 to 4 days
  • Shampoo mainly at hair roots
  • Deep condition for 10 to 15 minutes
  • Co-wash (wash with conditioner only) once a week, in place of shampoo
  • Colmb from the tips of your hair and slowly work your way up your hair to your roots
  • Detangle knots with your fingers and a wide-tooth colmb
  • Spray leave-in conditioner to moisturize medium to high porosity hair (hair that soaks up a lot of water)

3C Hair Texture

3C hair is characterized by loose, small coils. The coils each tend to be the size of a straw and the body of the hair is voluminous and puffy. General hair care for 3A hair includes:

  • Wash hair every 4 to 5 days
  • Shampoo mainly at hair roots
  • Deep condition for 10 to 15 minutes
  • Co-wash (wash with conditioner only) once a week, in place of shampoo
  • Colmb from the tips of your hair and slowly work your way up your hair to your roots
  • Detangle knots with your fingers and a wide-tooth colmb
  • Apply a "curl refresher," which is a type of leave-in conditioner made for 3C hair

4A Hair Texture

4A hair is characterized by tight, small coils. Each coil tends to be slim and defined and the body of the hair is compact. General hair care for 3A hair includes:

  • Wash hair every 4 to 5 days
  • Shampoo mainly at hair roots
  • Deep condition for 25 to 30 minutes
  • Co-wash (wash with conditioner only) once a week, in place of shampoo
  • Colmb from the tips of your hair and slowly work your way up your hair to your roots
  • Detangle knots with your fingers and a wide-tooth colmb
  • Use a leave in conditioner and also shea butter on the ends of your hair to limit fairy knots (one strand of hair knotted on itself)

4B Hair Texture

4B hair is characterized by tight, Z-shaped kinks. These kinks may or may not coil together and the body of the hair is compact and bouyant. General hair care for 3A hair includes:

  • Wash hair once or twice a week
  • Shampoo mainly at hair roots
  • Deep condition for 30 minutes or more
  • Co-wash (wash with conditioner only) once a week, in place of shampoo
  • Colmb from the tips of your hair and slowly work your way up your hair to your roots
  • Detangle knots with your fingers and a wide-tooth colmb
  • Use a leave in conditioner, put shea butter on the ends of your hair to limit fairy knots (one strand of hair knotted on itself) and do hot oil treatments as needed

4C Hair Texture

4C hair is characterized by tight, Z-shaped kinks. Each kink tends to hug each other firmly and the body of the hair is compact and bouyant. General hair care for 3A hair includes:

  • Wash hair once or twice a week
  • Shampoo mainly at hair roots
  • Deep condition for 30 minutes or more
  • Co-wash (wash with conditioner only) once a week, in place of shampoo
  • Colmb from the tips of your hair and slowly work your way up your hair to your roots
  • Detangle knots with your fingers and a wide-tooth colmb
  • Use a leave in conditioner, shea butter on the ends of your hair to limit fairy knots (one strand of hair knotted on itself) and do hot oil treatments
